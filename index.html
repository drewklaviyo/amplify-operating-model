Monthly Execution Review (MER)
Last week of month 60 min
Amplify leadership + all PMs + Eng leads

The "state of Amplify" meeting. Deep review of progress against quarterly goals, organized by partner org. What shipped, metrics movement, what's at risk. Goal updates must be written before this meeting — the MER is a discussion of them, not a place to present them for the first time.

Monthly Partner Digest
After MER Async
PMs → partner stakeholders

One-page summary per partner org, posted to #amplify-x-[org] Slack channels. Generated from your goal update — not a separate artifact. What shipped, what's next, metrics, asks/blockers.

The monthly flow

Weekly project updates (Fridays) accumulate context all month. Goal updates synthesize them into a narrative. The MER is the discussion. The Partner Digest is the broadcast. No extra prep at any step.

The Rules

Non-negotiable standards for every Amplify PM.

Rule 1
Every project ties to a goal

No orphan projects. If your project doesn't connect to one of the goals under "Working AI First," it either needs to be connected or it shouldn't exist.

Rule 2
Linear is the source. Everything else is a view.

If you present a roadmap in slides, it's generated from Linear — not maintained separately. When things change, you update Linear, not a slide.

Rule 3
Projects need milestones

At least one milestone per quarter per project. Name them clearly (e.g., "BDR Intelligence POC — Go/No-Go" not "M1").

Rule 4
Friday project updates are non-negotiable

Every Friday. Every active project. 4:00 PM calendar block. 10 minutes. Metric impact, blockers, next week. Set the health indicator.

Rule 5
Goal updates come before MER, not after

Write your goal-level status update before the Monthly Execution Review. The MER discusses your update — it's not the place to present it for the first time.

Rule 6
Issues go in the right team

Work for Sales goes in Amplify Sales. Work for Support goes in Amplify Support. If an issue spans teams, put it in the primary team and link related issues.

Rule 7
Use cycles consistently

Every active issue should be in the current cycle. Don't let issues float in backlog while engineers are actually working on them.
